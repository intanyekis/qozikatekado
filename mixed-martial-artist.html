Mark Muñoz, also known as The Filipino Wrecking Machine, is a Filipino-American mixed martial artist with an estimated net worth of $400 thousand.

Mark Muñoz started his career at the age of 13. He participated in the varsity team of Vallejo High School, and received numerous accolades such as two-time  State Champion, Asics First Team All-American, National High School Champion in 1995, and 1996 NHSCA National High School Champion.

Then, he got silver medal for the USA Junior National Team, and placed fifth during the FILA Junior World Wrestling Championship in 1996. And while Munoz studying at the Oklahoma State University, he got 121 wins and won the NCAA National Championship in 2001.

Munoz made a big switch from being a wrestler to a mixed martial artist. He debuted during the WEC 34 in 2008. Then, when he was chosen to be relocated to UFC, his first fight in UFC 96 was his first loss against Matt Hamill.

He faced several fighters such as Gegard Mousasi, Chris Weidman, and Demian Maia, among others. Munoz extends his contract with a four-fight deal for Ultimate Fighting Championship on June 12, 2014.

Mark Kenery Munoz was born on February 9, 1978 in Yokosuka, Japan. He graduated from Oklahoma State University with a degree in Health Science. He is married to Kristi and has four children.
