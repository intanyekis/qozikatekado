Rexha at a young age played trumpet and taught herself to play guitar and piano. She went to Tottenville High School on Staten Island, where she took part in a variety of musicals. She also became a member of the choir, while still in high school. Upon joining the choir, she discovered that her voice was a coloratura soprano. Rexha’s musical influences include Coldplay, the Cranberries, Lauryn Hill, Alanis Morissette, and Kanye West.

She submitted a song to be performed at the National Academy of Recording Arts & Sciences’ annual “Grammy Day” event and it earned her the “Best Teen Songwriter” award, beating around 700 other entrants. She, as a result, signed a contract with talent scout Samantha Cox, who encouraged Rexha to enroll in songwriting classes in New York City.

Rexha met Fall Out Boy’s bassist, Pete Wentz, with whom she began working at a recording studio in New York City in 2010. She later became the lead vocalist of Wentz’s new experimental project of a band, called Black Cards. They played a variety of live shows and released several singles and remixes. However, Wentz announced in January 2012, that Rexha had left the band to pursue other endeavors.

Bebe Rexha Monster

Rexha signed with Warner Bros. Records in 2013, as a solo artist. Rexha had already begun writing songs, which included Selena Gomez’s “Like a Champion” and Nikki Williams’s “Glowing”. Her 2013 most prominent songwriting effort was Eminem’s and Rihanna’s “The Monster”, which was released as the fourth single from Eminem’s album The Marshall Mathers LP 2. The song made it to the top of the charts for the US Billboard Hot 100 and Billboard’s Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs and won a Grammy Award for Best Rap/Sung Performance at the 57th Annual Grammy Awards ceremony. Rexha also wrote and was featured on Cash Cash’s single “Take Me Home” that same year.

Rexha released her debut single, I Can’t Stop Drinking About You, on March 21, 2014. The song peaked on Billboard’s Top Heatseekers chart, as number 22. Later on August 12, 2014, the music video was released. The music video was inspired by imagery from films such as Girl, Interrupted, and Melancholia. Rexha was featured on rapper Pitbull’s song “This Is Not a Drill” in November 2014 and in September 2014, she was picked as Elvis Duran’s Artist of the Month and was featured on NBC’s Today show hosted by Kathie Lee Gifford and Hoda Kotb, where she performed live her single “I Can’t Stop Drinking About You”.

Bebe Rexha And Nicki Minaj

She later co-wrote, featured on David Guetta’s single Hey Mama, alongside Nicki Minaj and Afrojack. The song which received 1.1 million downloads as of June 2015, peaked at number eight on the Billboard Hot 100. Originally the song did not credit Rexha, despite the fact that she sings the chorus and is featured on background vocals. She was eventually, in June 2015, given a credit for her work.

G Eazy Bebe Rexha

Rexha in January 2015, co-wrote and featured on G-Eazy’s “Me, Myself & I”. The song peaked on the Billboard Hot 100 at number seven and at number one on Billboard Pop Songs. The song originally titled I Don’t Need Anything, was intended as a song for Rexha herself but instead, she brought the song idea to G-Eazy and was featured during the chorus.

Rexha released her single, called No Broken Hearts featuring Nicki Minaj, in March 2016. The music video was released, in April 2016, directed by Dave Meyer. The video amassed over 240 million views on YouTube.

Martin Garrix Bebe Rexha

Rexha and Dutch DJ and record producer Martin Garrix released their single, In the Name of Love, on July 29, 2016. The song peaked at number 24 on the US Billboard Hot 100, at number four on US Hot Dance/Electronic Songs, and entered the top 10 in several countries, including the United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, Italy, and New Zealand. The music video was later released on Martin Garrix’s YouTube channel on August 23, 2016.

Bebe Rexha I Got U

Rexha hosted the 2016 MTV Europe Music Awards, at Rotterdam, Netherlands on November 6, 2016, and performed multiple songs throughout the night, such as her single, “I Got You”.

Bebe Rexha Expectations

Rexha began teasing new songs for a third installment in the All Your Fault series, in September 2017, with her manager going on record about its release. It, however, appeared plans had changed, as Bebe revealed through a tweet in November 2017 that her next project would be called Expectations. On April 8, 2018, Rexha revealed the cover art for this debut studio album, and the album was released on June 22, 2018. I Got You and Meant to Be previous singles from All Your Fault, appear on Expectations as well.

Ferrari and 2 Souls on Fire, the latter of which features Quavo of Migos, were released on April 13, 2018, as promotional singles along with the pre-order. I’m a Mess” was released on June 15, 2018, as the first single from the album.
