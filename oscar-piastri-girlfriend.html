Oscar Piastri Career

Career: - Oscar began his karting career in 2011 after racing remote-controlled vehicles on a national level. In 2015, he joined Ricky Flynn Motorsport and began participating in European and foreign CIK-FIA-sanctioned karting competitions. He relocated to the United Kingdom after a year to develop his racing career. In 2016, he received his first big sponsorship from HP Tuners (created and controlled by his father), which helped to support his racing career. Subsequently, with Dragon F4, he made his single-seater debut in selected rounds of the Formula 4 UAE Championship, collecting two podiums and finishing sixth overall.

In 2017, he was nominated to the TRS Arden Junior Team's line-up for the British F4 championship. In 2018, he made his championship debut. In December 2018, it was revealed that he will be joining reigning team champions R-ace GP for the 2019 season.

He won his debut race in the series at Silverstone and then won again the next day on the same track. He also became the first driver in 2019 to win three races after winning in Spa-Francorchamps in July, and he was crowned champion after winning and finishing fourth in the final round at Yas Marina.

In October 2019, he joined the post-season test with Prema Racing, the defending FIA Formula 3 team champions. He declared in December 2020 that he will stay with Prema in Formula 2. With R-ace GP, he won the 2019 Formula Renault Eurocup, and with Prema Racing, he won the 2020 FIA Formula 3 Championship and the 2021 Formula 2 Championship.
