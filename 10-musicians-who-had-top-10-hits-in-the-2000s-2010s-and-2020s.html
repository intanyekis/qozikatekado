10 Musicians Who Had Top 10 Hits In The 2000s, 2010s, And 2020s

September 2024 · 5 minute read

While there is no doubt that each decade tends to have its most successful musicians — the truly talented stars tend to stick around for more than just 10 years. Today, we're taking a look at those artists who have managed to have top 10 hits in the Billboard Hot 100 over the course of three decades — the 2000s, 2010s, and 2020s. Frankly, we wouldn't be surprised if some of these end up creating top 10 hits in the 2030s too!

From pop divas like Mariah Carey and Lady Gaga to rap stars like Eminem and Drake — keep on scrolling to see just which popular musicians have released successful music in different decades!

10 Taylor Swift

We're kicking the list off with popstar Taylor Swift. In the 2000s Taylor already had quite a few top 10 hits such as "Change" and "You Belong With Me". In the 2010s, Taylor Swift pretty much dominated the pop industry — and some of the hits that made it to the top 10 are "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together," "Shake It Off," "Blank Space," "Bad Blood," and "Look What You Made Me Do". This decade it seems as if Taylor Swift has continued her success with music and one of her top 10 hits was the song "Cardigan."

9 Mariah Carey

Next on the list is pop diva Mariah Carey who was also having top 10 hits in the 90s — so technically she has been successful in the charts for four decades. In the 2000s, Mariah made it into the top 10 with hits like "Thank God I Found You," "We Belong Together," "Don't Forget About Us," and "Touch My Body." In the 2010s and 2020s, Mariah made it into the top 10 with the same hit — the holiday anthem "All I Want For Christmas Is You."

8 Drake

Let's move on to Canadian musician Drake. In the 2000s Drake scored his first top 10 hit with the song "Best I Ever Had" in 2009.

In the 2010s, he was in the top 10 with hits like "God's Plan," "Nice For What," and "In My Feelings." In the 2020s the star's song "Toosie Slide" debuted atop the Hot 100.

7 Lady Gaga

Popstar Lady Gaga is next on today's list. In the 2000s she had plenty of success with hits like "Just Dance" and "Poker Face." In the 2010s, Lady Gaga was in the top 10 with songs like "Born This Way" and "Shallow." At the beginning of this decade, the musician also made it in the top 10 with her Chromatica singles "Stupid Love" and "Rain on Me."

6 Beyonce

While Beyonce had some top 10 hits with her former band Destiny's Child in the 90s — it wasn't until the early 2000s that the star charted the Hot 100 on her own. At the time, Beyonce made it into the top 10 with hits like "Crazy in Love" and "Baby Boy." In the 2010s, Beyonce topped the charts together with Ed Sheeran as she was featured on his hit "Perfect." In the 2020s, Beyonce managed to get in the top 10 as a feature on Megan Thee Stallion's song "Savage."

5 Jason Derulo

Next on the list is Jason Derulo who was in the top 10 of the Hot 100 back in the late 2000s with his hit "Whatcha Say." In the 2010s he had quite a lot of success with his 2014 song "Talk Dirty" — and in the 2020s Jason Derulo managed to get into the top 10 of the Hot 100 with his Jawsh 365 collab "Savage Love (Laxed Beat - Siren)."

4 Maroon 5

Let's move on to the band Maroon 5. In the 2000s they had Hot 100 top 10 hits with songs like "This Love" and "Makes Me Wonder."

In the 2010s, they topped the charts with the songs "Moves Like Jagger," "One More Night," and "Girls Like You." This decade the band once again proved that they are on top of their game by entering the top 10 with their hit "Memories."

3 Eminem

Even though rapper Eminem rose to fame in the late 90s it wasn't until the early 2000s that he had his first top 10 hit. That decade, he released hits like "The Real Slim Shady," "Lose Yourself," and "Crack a Bottle." In the 2010s he topped the charts with songs like "Not Afraid," "Love the Way You Lie," and "The Monster." Finally, this decade he entered the top 10 of the Hot 100 with his hit "Godzilla."

2 Chris Brown

Let's move on to musician Chris Brown. In the 2000s he had top 10 hist like "Run It!" and "Kiss Kiss." In the next decade, Chris had quite a lot of success with the "No Guidance" featuring Drake and in the 2020s he entered the top 10 with the Young Thug collab "Go Crazy."

1 Lil Wayne

And finally, wrapping the list up is rapper Lil Wayne. In the 2000s he had plenty of top 10 hits like "Lollipop" and "Down." In the 2010s, Lil Wayne found success by being featured turn on DJ Khaled's "I'm the One" alongside Justin Bieber, Chance the Rapper, and Quavo. In the 2020a he once again entered the top 10 on the remix of Jack Harlow's "Whats Poppin."
