Austin Theory

Austin White (born August 2,1997, McDonough, Georgia, United States) is a professional wrestler from America. He currently works for WWE and performs under the ring name Austin Theory on RAW. Before joining WWE, Theory competed in independent wrestling, winning the WWN Championship, the FIP World Heavyweight Championship, and Evolve Championship while competing for several organisations covered by the World Wrestling Network.

When he was younger, he was a fan of professional wrestling and looked up to John Cena as a major inspiration, and harbored dreams of joining the WWE since the tender age of 8. He finished first in the Teen Men division of the NPC Georgia Bodybuilding Championship in 2015 when he was 17 years old.

United States Champion

After Theory pinned Finn Bálor the night before on Raw, The Usos and RK-Bro were beaten by Theory and The Usos, giving them the opportunity to contend for Bálor’s United States Championship. He became the second-youngest United States Champion in WWE history after defeating Finn Bálor to capture the title.

Bobby Lashley defeated Theory for the United States title at Money in the Bank on July 2, ending Theory’s reign. Later that evening, he participated in and won the Men’s Money in the Bank ladder match, becoming the first Money in the Bank winner to lose a title and earn the contract on the same night and the youngest superstar in WWE history to do so.

At Survivor Series WarGames on November 26, Theory won the United States Championship for the second time in his career by defeating Seth Rollins and Bobby Lashley in a triple threat match.
