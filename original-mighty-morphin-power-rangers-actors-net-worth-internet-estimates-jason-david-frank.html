Here's How Much The Original Power Rangers Actors Are Worth Today (According To The Internet)

June 2024 · 5 minute read

Mighty Morphin Power Rangers was a show that undoubtedly marked the lives of the '90s and early 2000s kids. The show follows the adventures of a group of "teenagers with attitude" who are chosen to fight the evil alien sorceress Rita Repulsa, who has accidentally been released from her confinement and is threatening the Earth. The kids are given the ability to transform into a fighting force, the Power Rangers, and with that, they became the heroes of a generation of kids.

But what's been of the actors that made the show possible? While most of them has continued pursuing their careers, they haven't had such high profile roles since the show ended. Here's how much the internet thinks they are currently worth.

6 Austin St. John - $300,000

Austin St. John was the actor that portrayed Jason Lee Scott, aka the Red Ranger, the leader of the team of Power Rangers. He's one of the originals and wears that title proudly. He loved being a part of the show, and fans can see that if they go to his Instagram page, where he added "Jason, the Red Ranger" as part of his bio. Apart from being a great actor, he's also a martial artist and a paramedic, so needless to say, he's a man of many talents. Some of his other projects include the movies Expose and A Walk with Grace, and he also dabbled into writing in the '90s, when he published his book Karate Warrior: A Beginner's Guide to Martial Arts. His estimated net worth is $300 thousand.

5 Amy Jo Johnson - $300,000

Amy Jo Johnson, the woman in charge of bringing Kimberly Hart, the Pink Ranger, to life, has had quite an impressive career after she left her role in Mighty Morphin Power Rangers, but the show will always have a special place in her heart.

"It was such a gift that fell into my lap," she said about it. "It’s really cool how positively it affected so many kids. I hear stories from people whose parents were going through a divorce and it was their reprieve from that pain. I met this guy named Matt at the last convention I was at. He said when he was a kid, Power Rangers was his best friend because he was so bullied. Then he looks at me and he goes, 'Thank you for being there before I knew how to laugh.' It was just the sweetest thing anybody has ever said to me."

She went on to star in Disney Channel's show Susie Q, she appeared in an episode of Saved by the Bell: The New Class, and worked in the movies Perfect Body and Without Limits. These are just some of the many important acting jobs she had, but she's not just an actress. She has also had a career as a director. She directed some short films like Bent and Lines, and then she did the 2017 movie The Space Between and Tammy's Always Dying in 2019. She's now said to have a $300 thousand net worth.

4 Paul Schrier - $400,000

Paul Schrier portrayed Bulk in the show, and his current estimated net worth is $400 thousand. While his role in Mighty Morphin Power Rangers is undoubtedly the most popular one of his career, he has built his net worth working on many other important projects.

He worked as a voiceover actor in anime series, namely Daigunder, where he voiced Bone Rex, Eagle Riders, as Ollie Keeawani, and Teknoman, voicing Teknoman Saber. He also voiced Flonk in Cartoon Network's Mighty Magiswords, appeared in two power rangers movies, and worked as a comic book artist on The Red Star.

3 Richard Steven Horvitz - $900,000

Richard Steven Horvitz was the actor that voiced Alpha 5, the robot assistant fans love. His net worth is estimated to be $900 thousand, though some websites say that it might reach the million. Power Rangers wasn't the only popular kids show that Richard participated in. He voiced Billy in The Grim Adventures of Billy & Mandy, Grey Matter in Ben 10, Aviarius in Kim Possible, and Bumble in the videogame Kinectimals.

2 David Yost - Over $1 Million

While there isn't any specific information about how much David Yost, the man that portrayed the Blue Ranger Billy Cranston, is currently worth, his net worth is estimated to be of over a million dollars. His participation in the show, and in the high-grossing movies that followed, certainly contributed to him building his fortune. Right now, he makes a living not only from acting but also by selling cameo appearances and making Power Rangers merchandise.

He created the Affirmative Clothing Company, drawing from his character's catchphrase who used to reply "affirmative" instead of yes. It also had an important personal meaning related to his life experiences and his quest to accept himself.

"Everything I’ve gone through in my life in terms of being a gay man and the struggles that I’ve had, in terms of coming to terms with who I am," David explained. "If I am going to do something like a clothing line, I wanted to make sure there was a positive message behind it and that people would feel inspired by it or proud to wear the clothing that we’re making."

1 Jason David Frank - $1.2 Million

Jason David Frank started out in the show as the Green Ranger, but later moved on to portray the White Ranger. In Power Rangers Zeo, Jason played the Red Zeo Ranger, and in Power Rangers Turbo, he became the Red Turbo Ranger. He's not only a very accomplished actor, though. He also has a very successful career as a martial artist. He has mastered several different styles, including but not limited to Taekwondo, Judo, Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, and Muay Thai. He participated in a lot of competitions through the years and has won several accolades, including the 2003 title of Master of the Year (American Karate). According to Celebrity Net Worth, he's currently worth an estimated $1.2 million.
